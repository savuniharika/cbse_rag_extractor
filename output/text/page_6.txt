Examine whether ∆ABE and ∆ABF are congruent.
For this, you could use one or more of the following methods — tracing 
and comparing, taking a cutout and superimposing, or observing that AB 
acts as a line of symmetry due to the ‘sameness’ of the act of construction 
above and below this line.
We see that ∆ABE and ∆ABF are congruent. From this general 
construction, we can see that all triangles with the same sidelengths  are 
congruent. Hence, Meera was right when she said that the sidelengths 
are sufficient to construct a congruent triangle.
Thus, we have the following result:
If two triangles have the same sidelengths, then they are congruent.
We call this the SSS (Side Side Side) condition for congruence.
